It works by inhibiting the production of prostaglandins, substances in the body that cause pain, inflammation, and fever. Fever Reduction: Ibuprofen 400 mg tablets are also effective in reducing fever associated with common illnesses, infections, and inflammatory conditions. By reducing fever, they provide relief from discomfort and promote a more comfortable state. Trusted Manufacturer: Strava Healthcare Private Limited is a reputable pharmaceutical company known for its commitment to quality and patient safety. The Ibuprofen 400 mg BP Tablets are manufactured under strict quality control measures to ensure their efficacy.

Usage and Dosage: The tablets are designed for oral administration and should be taken as directed by a healthcare professional or according to the instructions on the packaging. The dosage and frequency of administration may vary depending on the individual's age, weight, and the severity of the condition. It is important to adhere to the recommended dosage and not exceed the maximum daily limit of Ibuprofen.

Precautions and Side Effects: Ibuprofen may have potential side effects, and it is important to be aware of them before using the medication. Common side effects may include gastrointestinal disturbances, such as stomach pain, nausea, and indigestion. It is important to consult a healthcare professional for guidance and to report any adverse reactions. Nitrostat® is a sublingual tablet that is used to treat Angina. Nitrostat is a form of nitroglycerin. After the tablet dissolves under your tongue, nitroglycerin is released. The nitroglycerin enters your bloodstream and helps improve blood flow to your heart.

Nitrostat is used to prevent or relieve chest pain caused by angina. Nitrostat may also be used for purposes not listed in this medication guide.

Angina is a pain or discomfort that feels like a pressure or squeezing in your chest. The pain may also spread to your shoulders, arms, neck, jaw, or back. Nitrostat can relieve the pain of angina quickly.

Composition: Feldene contains as active ingredient piroxicam 10 mg in the form of capsules and 20 mg/ml in the form of intramuscular injection solution (1ml ampoules).

Indications: Feldene is a non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug indicated in a variety of conditions requiring anti-inflammatory and/or analgesic action , such as: rheumatoid arthritis, juvenile rheumatoid arthritis , osteoarthritis (arthrosis, degenerative diseases of the joints), ankylopoietic spondylitis, acute musculoskeletal diseases , gout in the acute phase, pain generated by surgery or acute trauma, treatment of primary dysmenorrhea in patients aged 12 years and over and relief of fever and pain associated with inflammation of the upper respiratory tract.

Contraindications: Active peptic ulcer. Feldene should not be administered to patients who have previously shown hypersensitivity to the drug. There is a possibility of cross-sensitivity with acetylsalicylic acid and other non-steroidal anti-inflammatory agents. Feldene should not be used in patients in whom acetylsalicylic acid or other non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs cause symptoms of asthma, nasal polyposis , angioedema or urticaria .

Precautions: Feldene can rarely produce peptic ulcers, perforations and gastrointestinal bleeding, in rare cases fatal. Therefore, the administration of this drug in patients with a history of upper gastrointestinal diseases must be closely supervised. in rare cases, non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs can produce interstitial nephritis, glomerulitis, papillary necrosis and nephrotic syndrome. non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs inhibit the synthesis of renal prostaglandins that play a supporting role in maintaining renal perfusion in patients with low renal blood flow and blood volume. In these patients, the administration of non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs can precipitate renal decompensation, which is followed by the return to the pre-treatment state after stopping the therapy with these drugs. Patients at increased risk for such reactions are those with congestive heart failure, liver cirrhosis, nephrotic syndrome and manifest renal failure. These patients must be carefully monitored during treatment with non-steroidal anti-inflammatory agents. Due to possible adverse ophthalmic reactions of non-steroidal anti-inflammatory agents, it is recommended that patients who experience visual disturbances during treatment with Feldene consult an ophthalmologist. When used to relieve pain or inflammation in the upper respiratory tract, it should be known that non-steroidal anti-inflammatory agents are only a symptomatic treatment. If it is used in these cases, an appropriate antibiotic treatment must be taken into account at the same time.

Side effects: Feldene is generally well tolerated. The most common side effects encountered are gastrointestinal, but they usually do not lead to discontinuation of treatment. These side effects include stomatitis, nausea, anorexia, constipation, abdominal discomfort, flatulence, diarrhea, abdominal pain, indigestion. Gastrointestinal bleeding, perforations, ulcerations have also been reported with the administration of Feldene (see the chapter "Warnings and special precautions"). The objective evaluation of the appearance of the gastric mucosa and intestinal blood loss showed that 20 mg of Feldene per day, administered as a single or divided dose, is much less irritating to the gastrointestinal tract than acetylsalicylic acid. Long-term administration of a dose of 30 mg per day or more leads to an increased risk of gastrointestinal side effects. Edema was observed in a small percentage of patients, especially of the ankles. Effects on the central nervous system have rarely been observed: dizziness, headache, drowsiness, insomnia, depression, nervousness, hallucinations, mood changes, abnormal dreams, mental confusion, paresthesias and vertigo. Blurred vision, palpebral edema, eye irritation have also been reported. Routine ophthalmoscopy and slit-lamp examination revealed no ocular changes. Tinnitus may occur. Dermal hypersensitivity reactions, usually in the form of rash and itching, have been cited. Onycholysis and alopecia were rarely mentioned. Also, rarely, they have been associated with the treatment of photoallergic reactions. As with other non-steroidal anti-inflammatory agents, Lyell's disease and Stevens-Johnson syndrome may occur in rare cases. Vesiculobullous reactions were rarely cited.

Composition: One tablet contains propranolol 40 mg and excipients: lactose monohydrate Super-Tab, corn starch, povidone K 30, talc, magnesium stearate. Pharmacotherapeutic group: beta-blocking agents, non-selective beta-blockers.

Therapeutic indications Propranolol 40 mg is indicated in: hypertension; prophylaxis of angina pectoris attacks ; long-term treatment after acute myocardial infarction; treatment of heart rhythm disorders: sinus tachycardia, supraventricular tachyarrhythmias (paroxysmal tachycardia, atrial flutter and fibrillation, junctional tachycardia), ventricular arrhythmias (extrasystoles, ventricular tachycardia); it is of choice in arrhythmias of sympathoadrenergic pathogenesis; pheochromocytoma (in association with alpha-blockers); cardiovascular disorders in hyperthyroid patients; hypertrophic cardiomyopathy; migraine; essential tremor; states of anxiety due to sympathoadrenergic hyperactivity, accompanied by tachycardia, palpitations, tensional oscillations and tremors.

Contraindications: hypersensitivity to propranolol or any of the excipients; anaphylactic type reactions in the antecedents; bronchial asthma and chronic obstructive bronchopneumopathy; therapeutically uncontrolled heart failure; cardiogenic shock, marked arterial hypotension; sinus bradycardia ( 50 beats/min); sinus node disease (including sino-atrial block); atrioventricular block of degree II and III; Raynaud s type phenomena and other peripheral vasculospastic disorders; children under 6 years of age due to the pharmaceutical form.

Precautions: In case of symptomatic bradycardia (55 beats/min), the dose of propranolol should be reduced. Although treatment with beta-blockers reduces the risk of arrhythmias, myocardial ischemia and hypertensive jumps, during general anesthesia, the prevention of compensatory sympathoadrenergic reactions favors hypotensive accidents during anesthesia. When beta-blocker treatment cannot be interrupted (for example in coronary patients), it is recommended to protect against vagal predominance by administering atropine; general anesthetics that depress the heart will be avoided. It is recommended to inform the anesthesiologist if the patient is under treatment with beta-blockers. If it is necessary to interrupt this treatment, a break of 48 hours is sufficient for the reappearance of the response to catecholamines. The risk of anaphylactic reactions must be considered. In case of thyrotoxicosis, beta-blockers can mask cardiovascular signs in hyperthyroid patients. Athletes should be warned that propranolol and other beta-adrenergic blockers are included in the list of doping substances.

Interaction: The association with antiarrhythmics (amiodarone, propafenone, quinidine, disopyramide) can cause contractility, automatism and conductivity disorders by inhibiting compensatory sympathetic mechanisms. Beta-blockers reduce sympathetic cardiovascular compensatory reactions in the case of association with halogenated anesthetics. Simultaneous administration with calcium channel blockers (diltiazem, verapamil) increases the risk of myocardial depression; such an association requires great caution and is contraindicated in patients with cardiac risk. Propranolol increases the effect of antihypertensive drugs; for antihypertensives with central action (methyldopa, clonidine, reserpine) increases the bradycardic and conduction slowing effect. Tricyclic antidepressants, neuroleptics and baclofen administered simultaneously with propranolol increase the risk of orthostatic hypotension. Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) and glucocorticoids decrease the antihypertensive effect of propranolol by favoring hydrosaline retention. The association with insulin or oral antidiabetics requires blood glucose monitoring (hypoglycemic reactions are increased and prolonged, vegetative symptoms of hypoglycemia are masked). Treatment with beta-blockers should be stopped, if possible, before radiological investigations with iodinated contrast substances due to the risk of severe adverse reactions. Cimetidine increases the plasma concentration of propranolol by inhibiting its hepatic metabolism, increasing the risk of adverse reactions (for example marked bradycardia). Propranolol increases the plasma concentration of lidocaine by decreasing its hepatic metabolism, increasing the risk of adverse cardiac and neurological reactions of lidocaine. Enzyme inducers (for example phenobarbital, rifampicin) decrease the plasma concentration of propranolol by accelerating hepatic metabolism, diminishing its therapeutic effects. Magnesium, aluminum and calcium compounds decrease the digestive absorption of propranolol. An interval of 2-3 hours is recommended between the administration of these preparations and propranolol.

Special warnings: Treatment with propranolol should not be stopped suddenly in patients with ischemic heart disease, because it can lead to serious heart rhythm disorders, acute myocardial infarction or sudden death; doses are gradually reduced. If necessary, another antianginal drug is introduced during this period, to avoid aggravation of angina pectoris. In patients with vasculospastic angina (Prinzmetal), propranolol, like other beta-blockers, is not the medication of choice. It can be added to other antianginal drugs when they are not effective enough. In patients with therapeutically controlled heart failure, treatment should be initiated with low doses that can be gradually increased under medical supervision. Due to the negative dromotropic effect, beta-blockers should be administered with caution in patients with first-degree atrioventricular block. In patients with pheochromocytoma, alpha-blocker treatment must be instituted before the administration of beta-blockers, in order to prevent the risk of increased blood pressure. In the elderly, the treatment should be initiated with small doses and under careful medical supervision. Caution is recommended in setting doses in patients with liver and kidney failure. Diabetes monitoring is recommended; certain signs and symptoms that may indicate a hypoglycemic reaction may be masked (eg tachycardia, palpitations, sweating). The use of propranolol in patients with inadequately controlled diabetes requires caution. Prolonged administration of propranolol can cause an increase in triglycerides and, to a lesser extent, an increase in plasma cholesterol; the clinical significance of the increase in plasma lipids is not specified. When initiating treatment with beta-blockers in patients with psoriasis, cases of aggravation of the disease during treatment have been observed. In patients with a history of severe anaphylactic reactions, especially to iodinated contrast agents, as well as those under desensitization treatment, the administration of beta-blockers can be an aggravating factor and has an antagonistic effect on adrenaline.

If beta-blockers are administered to patients with liver cirrhosis and digestive bleeding, the blood count, hematocrit and hemoglobinemia must be checked periodically.
